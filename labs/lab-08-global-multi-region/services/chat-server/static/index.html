Lab 08: Global Multi-Region Distribution

This lab models two regional chat clusters with cross-region replication. Messages are published locally, then bridged across regions with event de-duplication to avoid loop storms.

Why this lab exists
Lab 07 handled social semantics. Lab 08 focuses on geography: when users are split across regions, chat events must propagate globally without introducing duplicate fan-out or infinite replication loops.
How replication works
Each region has its own Redis bus. A bridge service subscribes to both buses and forwards events by event_id. Nodes and bridge maintain short-lived seen-event maps to drop duplicates.
What to test
Open one tab on US and one on EU. Use the same room in both. Send from US and confirm EU receives with origin_region=us, and vice versa.
Observe metrics
Track bridge forwarded count, duplicate drops, and message latency. These reveal replication efficiency and whether global fan-out stays stable under load.
